Sky Lagoon

📍 Reykjavik

Experience the iconic 7-step Icelandic bathing ritual — from warm lagoon soaks to cold plunges, a scenic sauna, body scrub, and crowberry juice.

Tips
  • Bring a bathing suit for water activities
  • Eat a good breakfast before visiting to save on food/drinks
  • Carry shades/goggles and hair ties/clips
  • Use a waterproof phone cover to capture memories safely

Blue Lagoon

📍 Reykjavik

World-famous geothermal waters rich in minerals, known to rejuvenate skin. Enjoy the in-water bar, a silica mud mask, and a luxurious soak in this natural wonder.

Tips
  • Always bring a bathing suit
  • Apply conditioner before entering to protect your hair
  • Avoid dipping hair in water — minerals can make it dry
  • Shampoo and condition your hair properly after exiting

✨ Diksha's Pick

Whale Watching Tour

📍 Húsavík, North Iceland

Húsavík is known as the whale-watching capital of Iceland. The waters are home to humpback whales, orcas, and other species. A fantastic way to see these majestic creatures up close.

Tips
  • Dress warmly and in layers — the boat can get cold, especially in winter
  • Don't forget your camera to capture the whales!
  • Combine with a visit to the Húsavík Whale Museum for the full experience
Whale Watching Humpbacks Orcas Boat Tour

Adventure

Katla Ice Cave & Super Jeep Tour

📍 From Vik, South Iceland

Ride a super jeep to the Katla ice cave beneath the infamous Katla volcano. Explore stunning blue ice formations inside Sólheimajökull glacier. Only accessible during winter when the ice is stable.

Tips
  • Book in advance — tours are limited and weather-dependent
  • Dress warmly, it can be quite cold inside the caves
  • Carry food and drinks as nothing will be available once the tour starts

Full Day

Glacier Hike, Waterfalls & Black Sand Beach

📍 From Reykjavik

A packed day trip featuring a glacier hike, iconic Seljalandsfoss (walk behind it!) & Skogafoss waterfalls, and the dramatic Reynisfjara black sand beach with basalt columns.

Tips
  • Wear waterproof clothing — you'll get wet behind Seljalandsfoss
  • Bring gloves and a beanie, it gets extremely cold near the falls
  • Be careful of slippery rocks near the waterfalls
  • At Reynisfjara, avoid standing close to the water — sneaker waves are a risk

Experience the amazing aurora borealis on a northern lights tour from Reykjavik. Stay cozy by wrapping up in Icelandic wool blankets and enjoy hot chocolate and pastries as you admire the display.

  • Best chances are from September to March on clear nights

Seljalandsfoss Waterfall

📍 South Coast, Iceland

One of Iceland's most famous waterfalls with a unique feature: you can walk behind it! The waterfall cascades 60 meters down and offers stunning photo opportunities, especially during sunset.

Tips
  • Wear waterproof clothing or bring a raincoat — you'll get wet walking behind the waterfall
  • Wear gloves and a beanie — it gets extremely cold near the falls
  • Use waterproof covers for electronics to stay dry
  • Be careful of slippery rocks

Skogafoss Waterfall

📍 South Coast, Iceland

An iconic waterfall dropping 60 meters into a wide pool. Famous for its power and the rainbow that often appears on sunny days. Climb 370 steps to the viewing platform at the top for a panoramic view.

Tips
  • Climb the 370 steps to the viewing platform at the top for a panoramic view
  • Watch out for mist near the base — it can create slippery conditions
  • Wear waterproof clothing or bring a raincoat

Whale Museum

📍 Húsavík, North Iceland

The Whale Museum in Húsavík offers a comprehensive look at Iceland's rich whale-watching history. A great place to learn about the different species that inhabit Iceland's waters.

Tips
  • Combine your visit with a whale-watching tour for the full experience
  • Check the opening hours, as they may vary by season

Hallgrímskirkja

📍 Reykjavik

Reykjavik's most famous landmark, designed to resemble basalt lava columns found in Icelandic landscapes. Visit the tower for a 360-degree view of the city.

Tips
  • Visit early in the morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds
  • Don't miss the statue of Leif Erikson in front of the church

  • Bring binoculars for a better view of the wildlife

Kirkjufell

📍 Snæfellsnes Peninsula, Iceland

Often called "the most photographed mountain in Iceland," Kirkjufell stands tall near the coast. Its unique shape and picturesque surroundings make it a must-see.

Tips

  • Check the weather before visiting, as roads can be tricky in winter

Reynisfjara Beach & Lighthouse

📍 South Coast, Iceland

Known for its black sand, towering basalt columns, and the Reynisdrangar sea stacks. One of Iceland's most dramatic locations. The nearby lighthouse provides panoramic views of the coast.

Tips
  • Be cautious of strong waves, as they can be dangerous
  • Avoid standing too close to the water — sneaker waves are a risk

Dettifoss Waterfall

📍 Vatnajökull National Park, Northeast Iceland

Europe's most powerful waterfall. An awe-inspiring sight with water plunging down 44 meters. Featured in movies like Prometheus.

Tips
  • Wear sturdy shoes and be prepared for a bit of a hike to get the best view
  • The area can be quite misty, so protect your camera gear

Godafoss Waterfall

📍 North Iceland

Known as the "Waterfall of the Gods," one of Iceland's most beautiful and historically significant waterfalls, where ancient lawgivers threw their pagan idols into the river when they converted to Christianity.

Tips
  • Visit both sides of the waterfall for different perspectives
  • Bring a tripod for great photos
